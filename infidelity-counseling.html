How I think about infidelity

Affairs are both rupture and message

An affair is both a rupture and a message about the relationship's hopes, hurts, and constraints. We'll make space for both—without excusing harm.

Accountability over blame

Blame and "who's right" keep couples stuck. Accountability is a relational accomplishment we build together.

Both paths are honorable

Repair and separation are both honorable outcomes. Our aim is honest choice, not forced reconciliation.

Safety comes first

Safety and steadiness come first: clear boundaries, no secret side-conversations with the therapist, and no weaponized disclosures.

How we'll work (simple, structured, humane)

1

Stabilize

Slow the cycle. Name what's urgent. Agree on short-term safety rules (contact with third parties, phones, sleeping arrangements, disclosures).

2

Interrupt the debate

With your permission, I'll stop courtroom-style arguing and invite a different conversation—one that actually changes something.

3

Let the relationship speak

I'll ask questions about what your relationship is for, what each of you needs to do, and what goes wrong when you try.

4

Accountability over explanations

We'll map effects (on trust, intimacy, family) and positions. No moral lecturing; real responsibility.

5

Paths forward

If repairing: rituals of apology, transparency practices, rebuilding intimacy. If parting: dignity and clear agreements.

What I won't do

No judging

I won't act as judge, teacher, or referee.

No secrets

I won't keep secrets between partners.

No debates

I won't let sessions become about "who's right."

No quick fixes

I won't promise instant healing or easy solutions.
